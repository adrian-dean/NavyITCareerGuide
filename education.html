Education & Credentialing

Navy Platforms

  • Navy eLearning (NeL): IA Awareness, COMSEC, NEC refreshers, and PPME.
  • JKO: Cyber Awareness Challenge, OPSEC, and Joint Enabling training.
  • TWMS: Leadership, command-mandated courses, and mandatory GMTs.

Civilian Learning Platforms

  • Coursera: Google Cybersecurity, IBM Analyst, University of Maryland (Cybersecurity for Everyone)
  • edX: MIT, Harvard, WGU, and moreâ€”CS50, Intro to Networking, Cybersecurity MicroBachelors
  • Udemy: Practical courses such as Nathan House's Cyber Security series
  • Udacity: Nanodegrees in Security, Cloud, and Data Engineering
  • FutureLearn: International university courses with ECTS credits
  • NIST NICE/CDSE: Free CEU/DoD-endorsed security content

College Degree Programs

Many universities accept JST credit and provide flexible online degree options ideal for ITs and Cyber specialists:

  • Western Governors University (WGU): BS in Cybersecurity, BS in IT, MS in Cybersecurity & Information Assurance
  • American Military University (AMU): BA in Intelligence Studies, BS in Cybersecurity
  • Purdue University Global: BS in Information Technology, Cloud Computing & Security
  • UMGC (Univ. of Maryland Global Campus): BS in Computer Networks, MS in Cybersecurity Policy
  • Excelsior University: Accepts JST and CLEP for IT/Networking degrees
  • Southern New Hampshire University (SNHU): BS in IT Management or Cybersecurity

JST & College Credit Tips

  • Always request JST credit review during admission.
  • Use verified Coursera/edX certificates for ACE credit transfer.
  • Track CEUs and export eLearning transcripts from NeL and TWMS.
  • Submit External Training Request (ETR) for command approval if needed.
